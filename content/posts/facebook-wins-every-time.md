I was reading an article in
[Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/philippine-nobel-winner-ressa-calls-facebook-biased-against-facts-2021-10-09/)
about a statement given by the 2021 Nobel Peace Prize winner,
[Maria Ressa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maria_Ressa), saying
[Facebook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facebook) (FB) fails to protect against
the spread of hate and disinformation and is "biased against facts". Ressa lives
in the Philippine that are (per October 2021) the worldâ€™s biggest consumers of
social media, spending an average of 4 hours and 15 minutes per day using social
platforms, according to
[Datareportal](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2021-global-overview-report).
Ressa is a journalist in [Rappler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rappler),
Philippine online news website, that she co-founded and that actually started as
a FB page named
[MovePH](https://www.rappler.com/about/rappler-story-independent-journalism-impact)
in 2011, before later evoled into a standalone website. So, what is wrong with
using Facebook?

Before we can answer that exact question, lets take a close look at where
Facebook get its income from. It can't be from us users because Facebook is "free",
right? Well, it depends on what you are considering as a payment. Because as a
user you don't need to pay any [money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money) for
using FB, instead FB collects data about you and selling advertising space as
its primary income.

> "If you have no facts, you can't have truths, you can't have trust. If you
> don't have any of these, you don't have a democracy" - Maria Ressa
