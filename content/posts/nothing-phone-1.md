### Design

The design of the phone is where it really stand out of the crowd. The recycled aluminium sides and flat OLED screen look remarkably similar to an Apple iPhone. The build quality is very good, and the full HD 6.55in screen is excellent, with a big, bright, crisp and super-smooth, thanks to a 120Hz refresh rate.

### Camera
A main camera and an ultra-wide angle are what you get here. The phone has 2x zoom in the camera app, but then it is a selection of the 50 megapixels in the main camera that is used, and not a dedicated camera.

Here you get a decent night mode, and the back can also be used as a "ring light". The phone consistently takes decent pictures in most situations, regardless of which of the two cameras you choose. And for only 2x zoom, the resolution is also sufficient so that the lack of a dedicated zoom camera does not feel precarious.

Where the camera suffers the most is in color transitions and gradations worse than many other phones. Some colour banding occasionally occurs, where the gradations break down into bands of "main shades" without the smooth flow between different colors that I whish to see.

For the camera there is an "opponent". The Google Camera (gcam) application found in Google's Pixel series has been portred to work with the NP1. I therefor had to compare the stock camera to the gcam mod.
